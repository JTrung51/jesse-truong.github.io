A rubber duck is a small, floating toy typically made of rubber or plastic, often associated with bath time. Traditionally designed to resemble a bright yellow duck with an orange beak and friendly eyes, it has become an iconic symbol of childhood and relaxation. Beyond its role as a bath toy, rubber ducks hold cultural significance in various ways. They are used in "rubber duck debugging," a problem-solving technique where programmers explain their code to a rubber duck to uncover issues. Additionally, rubber duck races are popular charity events where numbered ducks are released into a river, with prizes awarded to the winners.
